# 1. Executive Summary

This compliance report evaluates a portfolio of 38 photovoltaic inverter models against the Nepal Electricity Authority's regulatory requirements for PV inverters. The models include two main series: CE-series (single-phase) and SUN-series (three-phase and single-phase variants). The assessment reveals that while many technical parameters such as output voltage, frequency, power factor, IP protection, and operational features are adequately documented and meet the requirements, critical efficiency metrics (inverter efficiency, euro efficiency, MPPT efficiency) and some other parameters (e.g., DC injection limit, flicker limit, no load loss, total harmonic distortion) are consistently marked as "NOT GIVEN" across all models. Consequently, no model fully satisfies all technical requirements. The SUN-series models demonstrate more comprehensive data coverage and compliance with operational and protection features, whereas the CE-series models lack efficiency and other key data. Overall, the compliance status is mixed with partial compliance on general and technical requirements but significant gaps in efficiency and performance data, leading to conditional pass or fail decisions depending on the model.

# Final Model-Wise Decision

| Model                      | Decision           | Justification                                                                                          |
|----------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| CE-series models (all 9)   | CONDITIONAL PASS / FAIL | Meet basic voltage, frequency, power factor, and IP protection requirements but lack critical efficiency, protection, and operational data. Without efficiency and other key data, full compliance cannot be confirmed. |
| SUN-series models (all 29) | CONDITIONAL PASS    | Provide comprehensive data on voltage, frequency, power factor, IP protection, operational features, and labeling. However, critical efficiency parameters (MPPT, inverter, euro efficiency), flicker, DC injection, and no load loss data are missing, preventing full pass. |

---

**Summary:** The SUN-series models demonstrate better compliance coverage and meet most general and operational requirements, but lack key efficiency and performance data. The CE-series models have more limited data and fail to demonstrate compliance on several critical parameters. Both series require submission of missing efficiency and performance data, IEC test certificates, and warranty documentation to achieve full compliance.
